The Devices That Make Networks Work

1

Meet the Router - The Network Traffic Controller

A router is like a traffic controller for data. It decides where information should go in a network.

  • Directs data: Sends information to the right device
  • Protects network: Keeps unwanted visitors out
  • Creates WiFi: Lets devices connect wirelessly
  • Internet gateway: Connects your network to the internet

Think of it like: A post office worker who reads addresses and sorts mail!

Router by DailyArt

2

Switches - The Network Connectors

What Do Switches Do?

üîå
More Connections

Switches add extra ports so more devices can connect with cables

üì®
Smart Delivery

They only send data to the device that needs it, not everyone

‚ö°
Fast Connection

Wired connections through switches are usually faster than WiFi

Think of it like: A power board that gives you more plugs, but for network cables!

3

Cables - The Network Highways

Fibre optic cable 2 by Digital Studio Limited

Two Types of Network Cables

Ethernet Cables
  • Use electrical signals
  • Made of copper wire
  • Good for most school networks
  • The thick cables you see in walls
Fibre Optic Cables
  • Use pulses of light!
  • Made of very thin glass
  • Super fast for long distances
  • Used for internet connections

Amazing fact: Fibre optic cables can carry data at the speed of light!

4

‚úÖ Advantages:

Most networks use BOTH wired and wireless connections!

How Information Travels Through Networks

üì¶ üì´ üöö

THE NETWORK POST OFFICE!

When you send a photo or video through a network, it doesn't travel as one big file!

Instead, it gets chopped up into tiny pieces called data packets!

1

What are Data Packets?

Think of data packets like sending a jigsaw puzzle through the post!

üß©

Step 1: Break It Up

Your big file (like a photo) gets split into lots of small packets - like breaking a jigsaw into pieces!

üìÆ

Step 2: Send Separately

Each packet travels independently through the network - like posting each puzzle piece in a separate envelope!

üîß

Step 3: Put Back Together

The receiving device reassembles all the packets back into your original file - like completing the jigsaw!

2

Why Use Packets?

Packets Make Networks Super Efficient!

Multiple Paths

Packets can take different routes through the network. If one path is busy, they can go another way!

Error Recovery

If one packet gets lost, only that small piece needs to be sent again - not the whole file!

Speed

Multiple packets can travel at the same time, making transfers much faster!

Sharing

The network can handle many files from different users at once!

Follow Data Through Your School Network

1

Real School Scenario

The Scenario

You're in Classroom A and you want to email a photo to your teacher, whose computer is in the School Office.

The Journey:
üì± Your Device ‚Üí üîÑ Classroom Switch ‚Üí üì° Main Router ‚Üí üîÑ Office Switch ‚Üí üíª Teacher's Computer

Remember: Your photo gets broken into packets and each packet follows this journey!

2

Packet Journey Steps

Step-by-Step Packet Journey

Step 1: Photo Preparation

Your device breaks the photo into small data packets. Each packet gets a label saying where it's going (teacher's computer) and which piece of the photo it contains.

Step 2: To the Classroom Switch

Packets travel through the ethernet cable to your classroom's switch. The switch reads the labels and knows these packets need to leave the classroom.

Step 3: Through the Main Router

The router is like a smart traffic controller. It reads each packet's destination and decides the best path to send it to the school office.

Step 4: To the Office Switch

Packets arrive at the office switch, which reads the labels and forwards them to your teacher's specific computer.

Step 5: Photo Rebuilt

Your teacher's computer receives all the packets and reassembles them back into your original photo. Email delivered!

‚ö° This all happens in less than a second!
