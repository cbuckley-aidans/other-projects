What is a Digital Network?

A digital network is a collection of digital devices that are connected together so they can communicate and share resources.

The Hidden Internet Highway

Underwater Internet Cables

The internet doesn't travel through satellites - it travels through cables on the ocean floor!

Amazing Facts About Submarine Cables
  • Over 400 cables connect continents around the world
  • They carry 99% of all international internet traffic
  • Each cable is about the width of a garden hose
  • They lie 3-8 kilometres deep on the ocean floor
  • Ships specially designed for this purpose lay the cables

Routers - The Network Traffic Directors

A router is the brain of a network. It decides where data should go and finds the best path to get it there.

  • Path finding: Works out the fastest route for data
  • Security: Protects the network from threats
  • WiFi creation: Provides wireless connectivity
  • Internet gateway: Connects your LAN to the WAN

Think of it like: A smart postal worker who reads addresses and chooses the fastest delivery route!

Router by DailyArt

Understanding WiFi Signals

WiFi and the Electromagnetic Spectrum

WiFi uses radio waves, which are part of the electromagnetic spectrum - the same spectrum that includes visible light, X-rays, and microwaves. WiFi operates at frequencies of 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz, which are invisible to our eyes but perfect for carrying digital data.

Fun fact: If we could see WiFi signals, they would appear as invisible waves constantly flowing around us, bouncing off walls and objects!

WiFi Signal Visualisation
Watch WiFi Signal Visualisation (Short)

WiFi signals spread out from the router like ripples in a pond, but they can be blocked or weakened by obstacles. Thick walls, metal objects, and distance all reduce signal strength, which is why some areas of your house might have slower internet or drop-outs.

Why signal varies: WiFi works best with a clear line of sight to the router. Each wall or floor it passes through weakens the signal.

Switches - The Network Multipliers

Network switch by FrancescoMilanese on Sketchfab

A switch is like adding more plug sockets to your network. It allows many devices to connect using ethernet cables.

What Switches Do
  • Expand connections: Turn 1 port into 8, 16, or more
  • Smart delivery: Only send data to the device that needs it
  • High speed: Usually faster than WiFi connections
  • Always on: No interference or signal drops

Think of it like: A power board that not only gives you more sockets, but also knows which device needs which electricity!

3

Network Cables - The Information Highways

Two Main Types of Network Cables

Ethernet Cables
  • Use electrical signals through copper wires
  • Perfect for connecting devices in the same building
  • Cheap and reliable
  • Maximum length about 100 metres
Fibre Optic Cables
  • Use pulses of light through glass fibres
  • Super fast for long distance connections
  • Connect cities and countries
  • Can travel thousands of kilometres

Amazing fact: Fibre optic cables carry data at nearly the speed of light - that's 300,000 kilometres per second!

Key Differences: LAN vs WAN Journeys

üè†

LAN Journey

  • Distance: Within one building
  • Speed: Very fast (milliseconds)
  • Ownership: All owned by your school
  • Path: Direct through local switches
  • Example: Computer ‚Üí Printer
üåç

WAN Journey

  • Distance: Across cities/countries
  • Speed: Still fast (fraction of a second)
  • Ownership: Multiple companies involved
  • Path: Through many networks and routers
  • Example: Computer ‚Üí Website

Both journeys happen automatically - routers work out the best path without you having to think about it!

Understanding Network Journeys

What We've Learned

  • Networks are made of physical hardware components
  • Data takes specific paths through routers, switches, and cables
  • Local (LAN) journeys stay within your building or organisation
  • Internet (WAN) journeys travel across the globe through multiple networks
  • Routers automatically choose the best path for your data
